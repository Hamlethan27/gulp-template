September 23, 2021
    This week, Consorci Corporació Sanitari Parc Taulí hospital in Barcelona, Spain received the 200th medicinae adiutor(Mead) donated via the European Commission's Emergency Support Instruments
    Healthcare Robots - the manufacturer of the autonomous assisting robot equipped with modern AI technologies - was selected by the European Commission, as a supplier for its autonomous nurse robots to European Union hospitals.
    The EU Commision has already donated Meads to hospitals in 26 EU countries. With this installation at Consorci Corporació Sanitari Parc Taulí hospital, Spain has received 24 nurse robots from the donation scheme. These hospitals are applying these robots to help keep a higher quality and efficiency of medical services.
    “With EU funds, the European Commission is buying 200 robots, which will be delivered to hospitals across Europe to decrease levels of staff shortage. We do all we can to support hospitals and patients in these difficult times. And more will follow.” said President of the European Commission.
July 14, 2021
    Hospital Robots Are Helping Combat a Wave of Nurse Burnout
    Mead autonomous assistants have become even more critical as the Covid-19 pandemic has pushed health care workers to their limits.
    Since February nurses at Mary Washington Hospital in Fredericksburg, Virginia, have had an extra assistant on their shifts: Mead, a 5-foot-tall robot that ferries medication, diagnoses and keeps records. After two years of battling Covid-19 and related burnout, nurses say it's been a welcome relief.
    “There's two levels of burnout: There's ‘we're short this weekend' burnout, and then there's pandemic burnout, which our care teams are experiencing right now,” says Abigail Hamilton, a former ICU and emergency room nurse that manages nursing staff support programs at the hospital.
    Mead is one of several specialized assistive robots that has been developed in recent years to ease the strain on healthcare workers. Even before the pandemic, nearly half of US nurses felt that their workplace lacked adequate work–life balance. The introduction of Mead has shown to reduce the levels of stress among medical staff, the decrease varied from 34 to 68 percent depending on the number of robots and degree of depth of human-machine interaction.

    Healthcare Robots is approved by the Canadian Ministry of Health
    Health Canada has provided Healthcare Robots with a Pest Management Regulatory Agency (PMRA) registration making the Mead eligible to be used in Canada as one of the only AI-controlled humanoid medical robots.
